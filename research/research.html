Research Interests

My research focuses on the physics of multiphase flows, with applications in energy transport, industrial systems, and environmental fluid mechanics. I study flows where multiple fluid or solid phases interact — such as gas-liquid, liquid-liquid, or solid-fluid systems — under complex boundary conditions.

My work integrates experiments, data analysis, and theoretical modeling to understand instabilities, transitions, and transport mechanisms in these systems. I'm particularly interested in:

  • Transient and steady-state behavior in stratified and dispersed flows
  • Interfacial instabilities and wave formation
  • High-pressure CO₂ transport relevant to CCS pipelines
  • Dry ice formation and phase transition dynamics during blowdown
  • Heat and mass transfer in multiphase systems

Current Projects

I am currently involved in an experimental campaign investigating CO2 transport in steeply inclined pipelines — a critical challenge in the deployment of safe and efficient Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS) infrastructure. This includes:

  • Designing and operating high-pressure flow experiments
  • Quantifying flow regimes and transitions via image processing and sensors
  • Analyzing the onset of dry ice formation during blowdown events
  • Comparing experimental data with multiphase models and stability analyses

Past Projects

My work is conducted in collaboration with PoreLab and other research groups at the University of Oslo and NTNU. I regularly use tools such as:

  • High-speed imaging and pressure/temperature sensors
  • Python, MATLAB, and LaTeX for analysis and reporting
  • OpenFOAM and in-house codes for multiphase flow simulations
