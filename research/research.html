Research Interests

My research focuses on the physics of multiphase flows, with applications in energy transport, industrial systems, and environmental fluid mechanics. I study flows where multiple fluid or solid phases interact — such as gas-liquid, liquid-liquid, or solid-fluid systems — under complex boundary conditions.

My work integrates experiments, data analysis, and theoretical modeling to understand instabilities, transitions, and transport mechanisms in these systems. I'm particularly interested in:

  • Transient and steady-state behavior in stratified and dispersed flows
  • Interfacial instabilities and wave formation
  • High-pressure CO₂ transport relevant to CCS pipelines
  • Dry ice formation and phase transition dynamics during blowdown
  • Heat and mass transfer in multiphase systems

Current Projects

I am currently working on two projects at the intersection of experimental fluid mechanics and carbon transport technology:

  • MUST (Multiphase flow in Steeply inclined pipes): Investigating flow regimes in high-pressure pipelines with the goal of developing scaling laws for performance metrics like pressure drop and holdup. My work includes dimensional analysis, experimental design, and real-time data acquisition using sensors and flow imaging.
  • ENCASE (Experimental investigation of CO₂ blowdown): Conducting research in France to understand the formation of dry ice during blowdown events. I focus on how impurities influence the triple point of CO₂ mixtures using precision measurements and optical diagnostics.

Past Projects

Before my current PhD work, I was involved in smaller research initiatives bridging wave mechanics, elasticity, and porous media flow:

  • Wave–Structure Interaction: Modeled the interactions between gravity waves and flexible elastic membranes floating on the ocean surface, exploring their dynamic stability and response under wave forcing.
  • PoreLab Methodology Development: Designed a particle tracking and velocimetry (PIV/PTV) method for studying multiphase flow in porous media, contributing to ongoing PoreLab research at UiO.

Across all projects, I employ tools such as Python, MATLAB, OpenFOAM, and high-speed imaging systems, alongside Coriolis and ultrasound flow meters, pressure/temperature sensors, and advanced diagnostics like gamma densitometry and x-ray imaging.
