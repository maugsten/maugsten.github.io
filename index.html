Welcome

I am a PhD Research Fellow in Fluid Mechanics at the University of Oslo, working broadly in the field of multiphase flow. My research focuses on understanding the dynamics that emerge when gas, liquid, and solid phases interact under realistic flow conditions.

I currently investigate gasâ€“liquid transport in steeply inclined pipelines, with particular relevance to Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS) applications. This work is carried out in collaboration with the laboratory team at IFE, where we operate high-pressure flow loops equipped with advanced instrumentation.

My academic background combines experimental methods with numerical and theoretical tools, including computational fluid dynamics (CFD), and machine learning (ML). I'm especially interested in applying fundamental fluid mechanics to real-world problems in energy, environmental, and process engineering.
